Background

About

Museums Victoria is a prominent organization that manages several state-owned museums in Melbourne, Victoria, Australia. As the largest museum organization in Australasia, its mission is to inspire visitors to explore the past, present, and future.

Main Problem

The main problem we aimed to solve was the underwhelming online presence of Museums Victoria. The existing website lacked explicit information about all the museums and their diverse exhibits, which led to missed opportunities for engagement and exploration. This resulted in a lack of awareness and understanding among potential visitors.

Target Users

Our primary focus was on tourists visiting Melbourne, both internationally and from interstate. We recognized that these users heavily rely on online resources and word-of-mouth recommendations to plan their visits, making the website's role crucial in driving their interest and decision-making process.

Necessity of Solving the Problem

It was essential to address this problem for tourists as they often lacked advice from family or friends like local visitors. Without access to comprehensive information about the museums and their unique offerings, tourists missed out on exploring these cultural gems before arriving in Melbourne, limiting their experience and appreciation of the city's cultural heritage.

Improving User Lives

The new solution sought to enhance the lives of users by providing them with an immersive and informative online platform. By revamping the website, we aimed to inspire curiosity, ignite wanderlust, and empower visitors to plan meaningful and memorable museum experiences.

This disparity between in-person and website attendance raises the question of why there is such a drastic difference. There are a couple of possible explanations for the disparity in attendance between in-person and website visits. One explanation could be the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic, which may have dissuaded some visitors from attending in-person.  However, another potential explanation is that visitors may have had difficulty finding the information they needed on the website. Despite high website traffic, lower in-person attendance could be due to a lack of clarity or accessibility of information on the website.

Evaluating for Usability

Objectives

Improve user experience

The website should be designed with a focus on user experience, making it easy for visitors to find the information they need, navigate the site, and interact with the museum.

Increase visitor engagement

The website should be designed to engage visitors and encourage them to explore the museum's offerings.

Enhance brand awareness

The website should be designed to showcase the museum's brand and promote its mission, vision, and values.

Increase revenue

The website should be designed to generate revenue. (online ticket sales, memberships, and merchandise sales as well as soliciting donations through the website)

UX Strategy

User research

Conduct user research to identify the needs, preferences, and pain points of the museums’ target audience.

Website's navigation system

Improve the website's navigation system to make it easier for visitors to find the information they need.

User-centric design

Develop a user-centric design that is intuitive, user-friendly, and accessible on multiple devices.

Usability testing

Conduct usability testing to gather feedback and identify areas for improvement in the website's design and functionality.

Results

Intuitive design interface

Participants praised the website's intuitive and user-friendly design interface. They found the layout, colors, and overall design to be clear, simple, and easy to navigate.

5 out of 5 participants

Relevant & accessible information

Clear and concise presentation of essential details, such as dates, locations, admission fees, and age suitability, was highly appreciated.

4 out of 5 participants

Visually pleasing / Engaging

Engaging visual elements, such as videos, high-quality images, and appealing colors, played a significant role in capturing participants' attention and sparking their interest to explore further.

5 out of 5 participants

Streamlined booking process

Participants highly valued a streamlined and efficient ticket booking process. They appreciated clear instructions, minimal steps, and the ability to easily select desired options.

5 out of 5 participants

Friction overcome regarding add-ons

Participants expressed no interest in additional extras during the booking process.  They found it slightly time-consuming and frustrating to decline unnecessary options.
